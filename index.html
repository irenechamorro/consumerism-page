HOLIDAY CONSUMERISM

This grapic design project was created during the fall of 2019, in the midst of the global pandemic

and online learning. Like most people, I was not feeling very optimistic about the upcoming holiday

season, with little chance of visiting my family in Peru, and even less chance of being able to

properly celebrate the holidays. With this in mind, I wanted to explore the meaning between the

holidays and the frenzied consumption we were used to, that had ultimately redifined the meaning

of the holiday season. In a class on creating icons that communicate and convey a message,

I decided to focus on a theme of the positives and negatives of the holidys, that resulted in 25

icons, one poster, and a childrens book design.

After sketching out all the icons and creating their final versions, we had to work on an overall

composition that was cohisive and fit in with our theme. My first color choice did not express

how heavy a message this project carried, so this part went through a couple variations.

The poster accompanying the icons had the same message as the icons above, but it is in a

format that is more versatile and more efficient. For the poster, I played on the monopoly

board game theme, to connect this with the excessive spending during the holiday season that

strengthens the hold of corporations.

The process for this was pretty straight-foward, most of the planning went towards choosing

a layout that most emersed the viewer into the game board.

The last part of this project a children's book about consumerism culture during the holidays,

where the characters in the book explain the excessive expenditure in simple terms.
